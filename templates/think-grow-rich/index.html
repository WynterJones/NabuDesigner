The Origin

Commissioned by the richest man alive.

In 1908, steel magnate Andrew Carnegie handed a young journalist named Napoleon Hill an extraordinary assignment — and no salary to do it. Interview the 500 most successful people in America, Carnegie said, and uncover the single philosophy that separates the rich from everyone else.

For the next twenty-five years Hill sat with Henry Ford, Thomas Edison, John D. Rockefeller, Theodore Roosevelt and hundreds more. What he found was not luck, talent, or inheritance — but a definite, repeatable way of thinking.

In 1937, at the depth of the Great Depression, he published it. Think and Grow Rich has never been out of print since.
